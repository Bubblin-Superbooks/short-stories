Even etymology supports me, for the very word crocodile means voracity. Crocodile—crocodillo_—is evidently an Italian word, dating perhaps from the Egyptian Pharaohs, and evidently derived from the French verb _croquer, which means to eat, to devour, in general to absorb nourishment. All these remarks I intend to deliver as my first lecture in Elena Ivanovna's salon when they take me there in the tank."
